A guideline towards key considerations when developing AI policies in higher education.

  • Most institutions adapt their policies reactively as challenges arise, often without a systematic framework.
  • This study bridges this gap by identifying core themes surrounding AI policies across the top 50 U.S. universities.
  • We leverage large language models (LLMs) to identify common and prevalent themes to save on labor and time resources.

1. Academic Integrity & Responsible AI Use

The majority of universities emphasize acceptance of use of AI in academic work, with a focus on preventing plagiarism and unauthorized assistance.

2. Clear Communication of AI Policies

Some institutions encourage faculty to articulate their expectations for AI use, ensuring that students understand the implications when using AI tools in their coursework

3. Data Privacy & Security Concerns

Many institutions place guidelines restricting the input of sensitive or confidential information into AI systems, stressing compliance with privacy regulations.

4. Ethical Considerations in AI Use

Many institutions acknowledge AIâ€™s potential for bias, misinformation, and perpetuating inequalities, with some encouraging critical engagement and ethical discussions on its use.

5. Continuous Adaptation & Policy Evolution

Some institutions commit to regularly reviewing and updating their guidelines to meet educational needs in the fast-evolving nature of AI.

6. Documentation & Transparency in AI Usage

Several universities require students and faculty to document their use of AI tools, ensuring transparency on how AI contributes to academic work. This includes providing prompts, outputs, and any modifications made to AI-generated content.

7. Instructor Discretion in AI Integration

Institutions offer varying flexibility for instructors to set AI usage policies when integrated into teaching, based on specific contexts and learning objectives.
